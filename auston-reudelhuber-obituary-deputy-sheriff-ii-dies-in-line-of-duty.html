Auston Reudelhuber Obituary, Deputy Sheriff II Dies In Line Of Duty

Publish date: 2024-08-29

Auston Reudelhuber: Remembering a Hero

In a heart-wrenching tragedy, the law enforcement community mourns the loss of Deputy Sheriff II Auston  Reudelhuber, a dedicated officer of the Forsyth County Sheriff’s Office. On a fateful morning, tragedy struck as Deputy Reudelhuber’s patrol vehicle was struck by a box truck, leading to his untimely passing. He was a 32-year-old deputy who had not only served his community but had also bravely served his country as an Army Reservist for six years.

Sheriff Bobby Kimbrough Jr. conveyed the heartbreaking news of Deputy Reudelhuber’s passing. He expressed profound sorrow and stated, “We are heartbroken to share that we have lost one of our own in the line of duty.” The entire community, together with the Forsyth County Sheriff’s Office Family, mourns the tragic loss of a true hero.

Deputy Reudelhuber was taken to a local hospital after the devastating collision, but the injuries sustained proved fatal. His service and dedication will forever be etched in the memories of those who knew and worked alongside him.

Joining the Forsyth County Sheriff’s Office on March 29, 2021, Deputy Reudelhuber was an invaluable part of the Field Services Division, where he held the role of a Field Training Officer. Prior to his tenure with the sheriff’s office, he dedicated three years of service to the Davidson County Sheriff’s Office. Notably, he served six years on active duty in the United States Army, continuing his service as a reservist after his active duty period.

The Davidson County Sheriff’s Office also expressed its heartfelt condolences, highlighting Deputy Reudelhuber’s heroism and acknowledging the profound grief experienced by both family and colleagues. The law enforcement community and the community at large come together to remember and honor Deputy Sheriff Auston Reudelhuber.

As the investigation into the tragic incident continues, it has been reported that William Benton, aged 24 and from Lexington, was operating the box truck involved in the collision. Charges against Benton include misdemeanor death by motor vehicle, reckless driving, and driving left of center.

Funeral arrangements for Deputy Sheriff Auston Reudelhuber are pending, and his memory will be honored with a patrol vehicle placed in front of the detention center on 201 North Church Street in Winston-Salem. This gesture will provide an opportunity for the community to pay their respects, leave cards, and share condolences with the Reudelhuber family during this difficult time.

The Winston-Salem Police Department and the Forsyth County Sheriff’s Office have expressed their heartfelt condolences, emphasizing the profound impact of this loss on the law enforcement community and the Reudelhuber family.
