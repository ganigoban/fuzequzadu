Dr. Max Gomez’s Passing Linked to Courageous Battle with Cancer

The world mourns the loss of Dr Max Gomez , a revered and beloved medical reporter for CBS New York, who departed on Saturday following an extended and valiant struggle with a formidable foe. At the age of 72, Dr. Gomez’s journey as a beacon of knowledge and inspiration in the realm of medical journalism came to a close.

Dr. Gomez’s courageous battle was against cancer, a relentless adversary he faced for several years with unwavering determination. Throughout his fight, he endured a series of medical interventions, including surgery, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy, sharing his personal health challenges, such as his weight loss, heart attack, and kidney transplant, with his dedicated viewers. His openness and resilience in the face of adversity marked him not only as a remarkable journalist but as an inspiring figure who utilized his medical expertise to inform and educate the public.

Beyond his distinguished career as a medical reporter, Dr. Gomez was a luminary in various fields, including authorship, scientific research, and education. His remarkable contributions garnered numerous awards and honors. Dr. Gomez’s dedication extended to serving on the national board of directors for the American Heart Association, the Princeton Alumni Weekly, and the Partnership for Afterschool Education. He was also a mentor to countless students and aspiring physicians interested in the field of medical journalism.

Dr. Max Gomez’s journey began in Cuba, where he was born, before relocating with his family to Miami. He graduated cum laude from Princeton University and earned his Ph.D. from the Wake Forest University School of Medicine. Fluent in Spanish, he was known for his ability to engage in meaningful conversations on a wide range of topics, endearing him as a friend, advisor, and confidant to many.
