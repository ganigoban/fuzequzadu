Selena Gomez Seems Completley Unfazed by The Weeknd's Songs About Her

Publish date: 2024-08-23

After The Weeknd dropped six emotional new songs last night, fans have been speculating whether the soul-baring lyrics about heartbreak and past relationships are targeted at Selena Gomez. But how does Gomez herself feel about her ex's musical shoutouts?

The singer and actress hasn't issued a direct response to the album (and she is by no means required to), but in her first Instagrams since its release, she seems completely unbothered. In fact, Gomez is all smiles in the shots, flaunting Coach's new Parker bag, likely in part of her partnership with the brand.

And she finishes with a subtle smirk:

No Weeknd clapbacks to see here.

Gomez and The Weeknd, née Abel Tesfaye, went their separate ways in October 2017, after about 10 months together. Selena reunited with longtime on-and-off boyfriend Justin Bieber shortly afterwards, but that rekindled romance reportedly extinguished this month.

The Tesfaye-Gomez-Bieber love triangle appears to be a big inspiration for The Weeknd's My Dear Melancholy album, and fans seem to agree. Listeners mainly pointed out the track "Call Out My Name" for being loaded with Selena references.

For example, there's the line, "I almost cut a piece of myself for your life," which is believed to allude to Gomez's kidney transplant last summer. And theres the lyric, "Guess I was just another pit stop / 'Til you made up your mind / You just wasted my time," which hints at Gomez's reuniting with Bieber following her split from The Weeknd.

Other tracks on the record also appear to reference their relationship, as well as Tesfaye's previous romance with Bella Hadid. Read through more of The Weeknd's cryptic lyrics here.
