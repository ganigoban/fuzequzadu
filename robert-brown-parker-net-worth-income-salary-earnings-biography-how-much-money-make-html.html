Robert Brown Parker (September 17, 1932 – January 18, 2010) was an American crime writer who lived from 1932 to 2010 in New York City. His most well-known works were the books in which he starred as the private investigator Spenser. In the mid-1980s, the ABC television network created the television series Spenser: For Hire, which was based on the character; a series of television movies based on the character were also made at the time. It is evident in his works that he has an encyclopaedic knowledge of the Boston metropolitan region. Parker died of a heart attack at the age of 77 at his home in Cambridge, Massachusetts, where he had been working on a book. His wife, Joan, found him at his desk, where he had been working on a novel. 

Critics and popular writers like as Robert Crais, Harlan Coben, and Dennis Lehane have credited Spenser’s books with not only inspiring their own writing, but also for revitalising and redefining the detective genre.
