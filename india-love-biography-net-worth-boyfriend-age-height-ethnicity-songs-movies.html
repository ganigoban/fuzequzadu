Early Life & Education

India Love was born on a chilly February 3, 1996, in the vibrant heart of Los Angeles, California. She bloomed within a lineage of digital pioneers, standing as the youngest star in a constellation of siblings known as the Westbrook sisters. This digital siblinghood includes Crystal Westbrooks, Bree Westbrooks, Morgan Westbrooks, Brooke Westbrooks, and the enigmatic Blake Westbrooks. 

Tied to this tapestry, her father, Warren Westbrooks, plays a role in the world of commerce, while her mother, Candy Westbrooks, infuses the essence of home.

From this familial embrace, India Love emerged with an affinity for the digital world. She embarked on her online journey via Tumblr, where her snapshots exhibited a unique allure paired with an individualistic style, captivating an ever-growing following.

Career

India Love‘s journey to the forefront of public attention was like a shooting star’s sudden blaze across the sky, catalyzed by her move from the expressive world of Tumblr to the expansive landscapes of Instagram and Twitter. Her digital footprints etched intricate patterns, inviting observers to traverse her world of photos, fashion choices, and daily chronicles. The outcome was nothing short of mesmerizing; her aesthetic allure, defined by distinctive tattoos and a gracefully contoured physique, captured hearts and triggered waves of appreciation.

The momentum of her digital crescendo effortlessly transposed into tangible opportunities in the entertainment realm. The visual medium of television unveiled her narrative through the reality show The Westbrooks, premiering on BET in 2015. Within the program’s frames, India Love and her siblings unfolded the tapestry of their lives, interweaving personal and professional aspirations against the backdrop of a digitally immersed existence.

Broadening the spectrum of her artistic palette, India Love boldly embraced the modeling world, adorning herself in collaborations with distinguished fashion houses. The glossy pages of publications such as Vibe and XXL eagerly captured her aura, casting her as an emblem of contemporary elegance. But her voyage was also marked by melodic overtures; singles like Loco and Candy on the Block marked her foray into music, an avenue she continues to traverse with resonating aspirations.
